Fossiliary: Stupendemys geographicus


This 9-scene comic strip was made for @dinosaurcomics 2020 contest and ended up winning 2nd place.
It focuses on the taxon Stupendemys geographicus, the largest known non-marine turtle, its habitat and behavior.

This work was an approach to a different medium with the aim for a more accessible and approachable format to the general public for science outreach. Most of it is based on the information from E. A. Cadena et al., 2020 and some references to the original description of R. Wood.

References

E.-A. Cadena et al., The anatomy, paleobiology, and evolutionary relationships of the largest extinct side-necked turtle.Sci. Adv. 6,eaay4593(2020).DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.aay4593

Wood, R. C. (1976). "Stupendemys geographicus, the world's largest turtle". Breviora. 436: 1–31.
